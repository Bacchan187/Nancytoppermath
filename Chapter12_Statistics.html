Chapter 12: Statistics

Mean, Median, Mode, Variance, and Standard Deviation

Definitions

1. Mean (Average)

Mean: Sum of all values divided by number of values

Mean = Σx / n

2. Median

Median: Middle value when data is arranged in order

For odd n: Middle value. For even n: Average of two middle values

3. Mode

Mode: Most frequently occurring value in dataset

4. Range

Range: Difference between highest and lowest values

Range = Max - Min

Important Formulas

1. Mean for grouped data: Mean = Σ(f × x) / Σf

2. Variance: σ² = Σ(x - mean)² / n

3. Standard Deviation: σ = √(Variance)

4. Coefficient of Variation: CV = (σ / mean) × 100%

Examples

Finding Mean, Median, Mode

Data: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10

Mean = (2+4+6+8+10)/5 = 30/5 = 6

Median = 6 (middle value)

Mode = No mode (all appear once)

Finding Variance

For data: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5

Mean = 3

Deviations: -2, -1, 0, 1, 2

Variance = (4+1+0+1+4)/5 = 10/5 = 2

SD = √2 ≈ 1.41

Mini Exam - Statistics

Q1: Mean of 2, 4, 6, 8, 10:
A) 4
B) 5
C) 6
D) 7
Answer: C) 6
Mean = 30/5 = 6
Q2: Median of 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7:
A) 3
B) 4
C) 5
D) 6
Answer: B) 4
Middle value of 7 values is 4th value
Q3: Mode of 1, 2, 2, 3, 3, 3, 4:
A) 1
B) 2
C) 3
D) 4
Answer: C) 3
3 appears most frequently (3 times)
