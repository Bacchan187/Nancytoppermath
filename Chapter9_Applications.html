Chapter 9: Some Applications of Trigonometry

Heights, Distances, Angles of Elevation and Depression

Definitions

1. Angle of Elevation

Angle of Elevation: Angle above the horizontal line when looking upward at an object.

Example: Looking at top of a building from ground level

2. Angle of Depression

Angle of Depression: Angle below the horizontal line when looking downward at an object.

Example: Looking down from top of building to ground

3. Line of Sight

Line of Sight: Straight line from observer's eye to the object being observed.

Key Formulas

1. tan(angle) = Height/Distance

2. For ladder/incline at angle θ with ground:

Height = length × sin θ

Distance = length × cos θ

Examples

Height and Distance Problem

A ladder of length 10m makes 60° angle with ground. Find height:

Height = 10 × sin 60° = 10 × (√3/2) = 5√3 m

Angle of Elevation Problem

From 100m away, angle of elevation to building top is 30°. Find height:

tan 30° = Height/100

1/√3 = Height/100

Height = 100/√3 m ≈ 57.74 m
