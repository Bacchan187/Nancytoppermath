Chapter 5: Arithmetic Progressions

Terms, Sum, Common Difference, and Applications

Definitions

1. Arithmetic Progression (AP)

AP: Sequence where difference between consecutive terms is constant.

General form: a, a+d, a+2d, a+3d, ...

where 'a' = first term, 'd' = common difference

2. Common Difference

d = aₙ - aₙ₋₁

Example: In 2, 5, 8, 11, ... d = 3

Important Formulas

1. nth Term: aₙ = a + (n-1)d

2. Sum of n terms (Sₙ):

Sₙ = n/2 × [2a + (n-1)d]

OR Sₙ = n/2 × (First term + Last term)

3. Last term (l): l = a + (n-1)d

Properties & Methods

Finding nth Term

Example: Find 10th term of AP: 2, 5, 8, 11, ...

a = 2, d = 3, n = 10

a₁₀ = 2 + (10-1) × 3 = 2 + 27 = 29

Finding Sum of n Terms

Example: Sum of first 20 terms of AP: 1, 4, 7, 10, ...

a = 1, d = 3, n = 20

S₂₀ = 20/2 × [2(1) + 19×3] = 10 × [2 + 57] = 10 × 59 = 590

Mini Exam - Arithmetic Progressions

Q1: 10th term of AP: 2, 5, 8, 11, ...
A) 27
B) 28
C) 29
D) 30
Answer: C) 29
a₁₀ = 2 + 9×3 = 29
Q2: Sum of first 20 terms of AP: 1, 4, 7, ...
A) 590
B) 610
C) 630
D) 650
Answer: A) 590
S₂₀ = 20/2 × [2 + 57] = 590
Q3: Is 41 a term of AP: 1, 5, 9, 13, ...?
A) Yes
B) No
C) Cannot determine
D) Maybe
Answer: A) Yes
41 = 1 + (n-1)×4 → n = 11
