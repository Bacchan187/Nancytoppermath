Chapter 6: Triangles

Similarity, Congruence, Pythagoras Theorem, and Applications

Definitions

1. Similar Triangles

Similar Triangles: Two triangles are similar if corresponding angles are equal and corresponding sides are proportional.

Notation: △ABC ~ △PQR

2. Congruent Triangles

Congruent Triangles: Two triangles are congruent if they have same shape and size.

Notation: △ABC ≅ △PQR

Important Theorems

1. Basic Proportionality Theorem (Thales' Theorem)

Statement: If a line parallel to one side of triangle intersects other two sides, it divides them proportionally.

If DE ∥ BC, then AD/DB = AE/EC

2. Pythagoras Theorem

Statement: In right triangle, square of hypotenuse = sum of squares of other sides.

Formula: c² = a² + b²

Example: (3-4-5) triangle, (5-12-13) triangle

Important Formulas

1. Area of triangle: A = 1/2 × base × height

2. Area using sides (Heron's formula):

s = (a + b + c)/2

A = √[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)]

3. Ratio of areas of similar triangles:

Ratio = (ratio of sides)²

Mini Exam - Triangles

Q1: In right triangle, a=3, b=4, find c:
A) 5
B) 6
C) 7
D) 8
Answer: A) 5
c² = 9 + 16 = 25 → c = 5
Q2: Similar triangles with sides 2:3 have area ratio:
A) 2:3
B) 4:9
C) 8:27
D) 1:3
Answer: B) 4:9
Area ratio = (2:3)² = 4:9
