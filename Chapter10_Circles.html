Chapter 10: Circles

Tangents, Chords, Secants, and Circle Properties

Definitions

1. Circle

Circle: Set of all points equidistant from a fixed point (center)

Equation: (x - h)² + (y - k)² = r²

2. Tangent

Tangent: Line touching circle at exactly one point

Property: Tangent is perpendicular to radius at point of contact

3. Chord and Secant

Chord: Line segment joining two points on circle

Secant: Line intersecting circle at two points

Important Theorems

Theorem 1: Tangent perpendicular to radius at point of contact

Theorem 2: Two tangents from external point are equal

Theorem 3: Perpendicular from center bisects the chord

Important Formulas

1. Circumference: C = 2πr

2. Area: A = πr²

3. Arc length: l = (θ/360°) × 2πr

Mini Exam - Circles

Q1: Circumference of circle with r=7cm:
A) 14π cm
B) 7π cm
C) 49π cm
D) 14 cm
Answer: A) 14π cm
C = 2πr = 2π(7) = 14π cm
Q2: Area of circle with r=5cm:
A) 10π cm²
B) 25π cm²
C) 50π cm²
D) 100π cm²
Answer: B) 25π cm²
A = πr² = π(5)² = 25π cm²
