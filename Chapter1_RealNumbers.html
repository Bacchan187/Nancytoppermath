Chapter 1: Real Numbers

Euclid's Division Lemma, HCF, LCM, and Properties of Real Numbers

Definitions

1. Real Numbers

Real Numbers: Set of all rational and irrational numbers. Denoted by ℝ.

Rational Numbers (Q): Numbers that can be expressed as p/q where p, q ∈ Z and q ≠ 0

Irrational Numbers: Numbers that cannot be expressed as p/q. Examples: √2, √3, π, e

2. Divisibility

Divisibility: If a and b are integers and b divides a (written as b|a), then there exists an integer k such that a = bk

3. Prime and Composite Numbers

Prime Number: Natural number > 1 that has exactly two divisors: 1 and itself. Examples: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13...

Composite Number: Natural number > 1 that has more than two divisors. Examples: 4, 6, 8, 9, 10...

Note: 1 is neither prime nor composite

4. HCF (Highest Common Factor)

HCF (GCD): The largest positive integer that divides both numbers. Example: HCF(12, 18) = 6

5. LCM (Least Common Multiple)

LCM: The smallest positive integer that is divisible by both numbers. Example: LCM(12, 18) = 36

Important Theorems

1. Euclid's Division Lemma

Statement: For any two positive integers a and b, there exist unique integers q (quotient) and r (remainder) such that:

a = bq + r, where 0 ≤ r < b

Proof Idea: Dividing a by b gives quotient q and remainder r

Application: Used to find HCF(a,b) by repeated division

2. Euclid's Division Algorithm

To find HCF(a,b) where a > b:

Step 1: a = bq₁ + r₁

Step 2: b = r₁q₂ + r₂

Step 3: r₁ = r₂q₃ + r₃

Continue until remainder = 0

HCF = Last non-zero remainder

3. Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic

Statement: Every composite number can be expressed uniquely as a product of prime numbers, except for the order of the primes.

Example: 84 = 2² × 3 × 7

Use: Finding HCF and LCM using prime factorization

4. HCF × LCM Relationship

HCF(a,b) × LCM(a,b) = a × b

Example: If a=12, b=18

HCF = 6, LCM = 36

6 × 36 = 216 = 12 × 18 ✓

Important Formulas

1. Euclid's Division Lemma: a = bq + r where 0 ≤ r < b

2. HCF × LCM = Product of numbers: HCF(a,b) × LCM(a,b) = a × b

3. Prime Factorization method for HCF: Take common prime factors with lowest powers

4. Prime Factorization method for LCM: Take all prime factors with highest powers

Methods

Finding HCF using Euclid's Algorithm

Example: Find HCF(48, 18)

Step 1: 48 = 18 × 2 + 12

Step 2: 18 = 12 × 1 + 6

Step 3: 12 = 6 × 2 + 0

∴ HCF(48, 18) = 6

Finding HCF and LCM using Prime Factorization

Example: Find HCF and LCM of 12 and 18

12 = 2² × 3

18 = 2 × 3²

HCF = 2¹ × 3¹ = 6

LCM = 2² × 3² = 36

Check: 6 × 36 = 216 = 12 × 18 ✓

Irrational Numbers

Proof that √2 is irrational:

Assume √2 = p/q (where p, q are coprime)

Then: 2 = p²/q² → p² = 2q²

This means p² is even → p is even

Let p = 2k, then 4k² = 2q² → q² = 2k²

This means q is also even

Contradiction! Hence √2 is irrational.

Mini Exam - Real Numbers

Q1: Find HCF(96, 64) using Euclid's Division Lemma
A) 16
B) 32
C) 8
D) 64
Answer: B) 32
96 = 64 × 1 + 32
64 = 32 × 2 + 0
∴ HCF(96, 64) = 32
Q2: If HCF(a,b) = 6 and LCM(a,b) = 60, find a × b
A) 180
B) 240
C) 360
D) 480
Answer: C) 360
Using HCF × LCM = a × b
6 × 60 = 360
Q3: LCM(12, 18) =
A) 36
B) 24
C) 18
D) 72
Answer: A) 36
12 = 2² × 3
18 = 2 × 3²
LCM = 2² × 3² = 36
Q4: Which of the following is irrational?
A) √4
B) √5
C) 22/7
D) 0.5
Answer: B) √5
√4 = 2 (rational)
√5 cannot be written as p/q (irrational)
22/7 is rational
0.5 = 1/2 (rational)
Q5: Prime factorization of 84 is:
A) 2 × 3 × 7
B) 2² × 3 × 7
C) 2 × 3² × 7
D) 2² × 3²
Answer: B) 2² × 3 × 7
84 = 4 × 21 = 4 × 3 × 7 = 2² × 3 × 7
