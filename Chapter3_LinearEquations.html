Chapter 3: Pair of Linear Equations in Two Variables

Methods of Solving: Substitution, Elimination, Cross-Multiplication, Graphical

Definitions

1. Linear Equation in Two Variables

Definition: Equation of form ax + by + c = 0 where a, b, c are real numbers and a, b are not both zero.

Standard form: ax + by = c

2. Pair of Linear Equations

Definition: System of two linear equations in two variables:

a₁x + b₁y + c₁ = 0

a₂x + b₂y + c₂ = 0

3. Types of Systems

  • Consistent: System has at least one solution
  • Inconsistent: System has no solution (parallel lines)
  • Independent: Unique solution (intersecting lines)
  • Dependent: Infinite solutions (same line)

Conditions for Solutions

Ratio Comparison Lines Type Solutions Type
a₁/a₂ ≠ b₁/b₂ Intersecting Unique Consistent & Independent
a₁/a₂ = b₁/b₂ = c₁/c₂ Coincident Infinite Consistent & Dependent
a₁/a₂ = b₁/b₂ ≠ c₁/c₂ Parallel None Inconsistent

Important Formulas & Methods

1. Substitution Method

Step 1: Solve one equation for one variable
Step 2: Substitute in other equation
Step 3: Solve for variable
Step 4: Back-substitute to find other variable

2. Elimination Method

Step 1: Make coefficients of one variable equal
Step 2: Add or subtract equations
Step 3: Solve for remaining variable
Step 4: Find other variable

3. Cross-Multiplication Method

For a₁x + b₁y + c₁ = 0 and a₂x + b₂y + c₂ = 0:
x/(b₁c₂ - b₂c₁) = y/(c₁a₂ - c₂a₁) = 1/(a₁b₂ - a₂b₁)

Examples

Using Substitution Method

Solve: 2x + y = 7 and x - y = 2

From equation 2: x = y + 2

Substitute in equation 1: 2(y + 2) + y = 7

2y + 4 + y = 7 → 3y = 3 → y = 1

x = 1 + 2 = 3

Solution: (3, 1)

Using Elimination Method

Solve: 3x + 2y = 11 and 2x - 3y = 3

Multiply 1st by 3: 9x + 6y = 33

Multiply 2nd by 2: 4x - 6y = 6

Add: 13x = 39 → x = 3

From 1st: 9 + 2y = 11 → y = 1

Solution: (3, 1)

Mini Exam - Linear Equations

Q1: Solve 2x + y = 7 and x - y = 2
A) (3, 1)
B) (2, 3)
C) (1, 5)
D) (4, -1)
Answer: A) (3, 1)
Adding equations: 3x = 9 → x = 3
From 2nd: 3 - y = 2 → y = 1
Q2: For 3x + 2y = 8 and 6x + 4y = 16, system is:
A) Unique solution
B) Infinite solutions
C) No solution
D) Cannot determine
Answer: B) Infinite solutions
Equation 2 = 2 × Equation 1 (Same line)
Q3: Number of solutions of x + 2y = 5 and x + 2y = 7
A) 0
B) 1
C) 2
D) Infinite
Answer: A) 0
Same coefficients but different constants
Parallel lines - No solution
