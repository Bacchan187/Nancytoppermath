Chapter 13: Probability

Events, Outcomes, Experimental & Theoretical Probability

Definitions

1. Probability

Probability: Measure of likelihood that an event will occur

Range: 0 ≤ P(E) ≤ 1

2. Sample Space

Sample Space (S): Set of all possible outcomes of an experiment

Example: For coin toss, S = {H, T}

3. Event

Event: Subset of sample space

Favorable Outcomes: Outcomes satisfying the event condition

4. Complement of Event

Complement (E'): Set of outcomes NOT in E

Property: P(E) + P(E') = 1

Important Formulas

1. Theoretical Probability:

P(E) = Number of favorable outcomes / Total number of outcomes

2. Experimental Probability:

P(E) = Number of times E occurred / Total number of trials

3. Complement Rule: P(E') = 1 - P(E)

4. Mutually Exclusive Events: P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B)

Examples

Coin Toss

Probability of getting head in coin toss:

Sample space = {H, T}

Favorable outcomes = {H} = 1

P(H) = 1/2 = 0.5

Dice Roll

Probability of rolling 6:

Sample space = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}

Favorable outcomes = {6} = 1

P(6) = 1/6 ≈ 0.167

Playing Cards

Probability of drawing red card:

Total cards = 52

Red cards = 26

P(Red) = 26/52 = 1/2

Mini Exam - Probability

Q1: Probability of getting head in coin toss:
A) 0
B) 1/4
C) 1/2
D) 1
Answer: C) 1/2
Two equally likely outcomes: H or T
Q2: Probability of rolling 6 on dice:
A) 1/6
B) 1/4
C) 1/3
D) 1/2
Answer: A) 1/6
Only one favorable outcome out of 6
Q3: Probability of NOT getting 6 on dice:
A) 1/6
B) 2/6
C) 5/6
D) 1
Answer: C) 5/6
P(not 6) = 1 - P(6) = 1 - 1/6 = 5/6
Q4: Drawing red card from deck:
A) 1/4
B) 1/2
C) 3/4
D) 1/52
Answer: B) 1/2
26 red out of 52 cards = 26/52 = 1/2
Q5: Range of Probability:
A) -1 to 1
B) 0 to 1
C) 0 to 100
D) Any real number
Answer: B) 0 to 1
0 ≤ P(E) ≤ 1 for any event
