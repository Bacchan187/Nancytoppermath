Chapter 4: Quadratic Equations

Solutions, Discriminant, Nature of Roots, and Applications

Definitions

1. Quadratic Equation

Definition: Equation of form ax² + bx + c = 0 where a ≠ 0, a, b, c are real.

Example: x² - 5x + 6 = 0, 2x² + 3x - 2 = 0

2. Root/Solution

Root: Value of x that satisfies the equation ax² + bx + c = 0

3. Discriminant

Discriminant (Δ): Δ = b² - 4ac

Determines the nature and number of roots

Important Formulas

1. Quadratic Formula:

x = (-b ± √(b² - 4ac)) / 2a

2. Discriminant: Δ = b² - 4ac

3. Sum of roots (α + β): -b/a

4. Product of roots (αβ): c/a

Nature of Roots

Discriminant Δ Nature of Roots Roots
Δ > 0 (Perfect square) Two distinct real roots Rational and unequal
Δ > 0 (Not perfect square) Two distinct real roots Irrational conjugates
Δ = 0 Two equal real roots Real and equal
Δ < 0 No real roots Imaginary/Complex

Methods of Solving

1. Factorization Method

Example: x² - 7x + 12 = 0

(x - 3)(x - 4) = 0

x = 3 or x = 4

2. Completing the Square

Example: x² - 4x - 5 = 0

x² - 4x = 5

x² - 4x + 4 = 5 + 4

(x - 2)² = 9

x - 2 = ±3

x = 5 or x = -1

3. Quadratic Formula

Example: 2x² - 3x - 5 = 0

a = 2, b = -3, c = -5

x = (3 ± √(9 + 40)) / 4 = (3 ± √49) / 4 = (3 ± 7) / 4

x = 10/4 = 5/2 or x = -4/4 = -1

Mini Exam - Quadratic Equations

Q1: Solve x² - 7x + 12 = 0
A) 3, 4
B) 2, 6
C) 1, 12
D) -3, 4
Answer: A) 3, 4
(x - 3)(x - 4) = 0
Q2: Nature of roots of x² - 4x + 4 = 0
A) Two distinct real
B) Two equal real
C) No real roots
D) Irrational
Answer: B) Two equal real
Δ = 16 - 16 = 0
Q3: Sum of roots of 3x² - 6x + 2 = 0
A) 2
B) -2
C) 6/3
D) -6/3
Answer: A) 2
Sum = -b/a = 6/3 = 2
